If you like that genre, check out Thievery Corporation, Morcheeba, Massive Attack and Portishead.

Thievery is probably my favorite. Their latest disc, Richest Man in Babylon, is amazing. If you ever have the chance to see them live, highly recommend it, it's a great show.

I've been generally regarded all around as a Metal Head, since I used to listen hard rock and heavy-metal (and from there upwards) almost exclusively. However, in the past few years, my music taste has broaden quite a bit, and now I tend to listen to a mix of genres, still featuring rock & metal promintently, too.

One of the music styles I've been acquiring a taste for is lounge-like music (chill out, ambience, etc.). In that vein, I picked up a copy of Watermusic's Excursions; a journey into chill out compilation CD a couple of days ago. I was quite impressed with it, and highly recommend it if you like the genre. My favorite piece so far is Lost Soul, by Bliss.

Last week I also picked up a copy of Garry Judd's Ambience album, which I really like, too. The best songs in the CD are definitely Coasting and Sheltering Sky.
